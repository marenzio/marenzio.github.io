1.1. Editorial methods

The editorial criteria conform to the General editorial criteria for MODE.

The occasional substitution of a segment of text with a repetition sign (or, much more rarely, vice versa) in the re-editions has not been recorded in the apparatus; in general, both AnGa-1603 and Ma-Ga-1614 tend to have more repetition signs than AnGa-1594.

A note on the equivalences in madrigal no. 16, Ecco Maggio seren, chi l’ha vestito. The piece, set in C, presents three passages in triple meter (mm. 8–17, 34–37, and 43–50), each introduced by the sign 3, with perfection at the level of semibrevis (groups of 3 minimae). As it is often the case in late sixteenth- and early seventeenth-century vocal music, a strictly proportional interpretation of the sign 3 seems to lead to unsatisfactory results, and it seems preferable to maintain a steady basic beat between duple and triple time sections (C 𝅘𝅥 = 3 𝅗𝅥 ). The equivalences provided in the score should be interpreted as an orientation from which performers can develop appropriate mensural relationships as they shift between duple and triple meters.

2.1. Editorial methods

The editorial criteria conform to the General editorial criteria for the MODE, except for the following:

- In no. 14, line 2 (“Ed io morir desio,”), I have rendered the Latinism et as ed (instead of e), maintaining the euphonic d, as it is customary for the phrase ed io.

- When in the score there is a structural repetition of the last two lines (as in nos. 1 and 3), I have maintained the capitalized first letter at the beginning of the repeated lines.

References to Contemporary editions are provided based primarily on Cecchi’s “List of Secular and Devotional Compositions by Luca Marenzio”1 and on RePIM (http://repim.muspe.unibo.it/), occasionally integrated with other available information, but without pretence of completeness.
