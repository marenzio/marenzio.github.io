According to the general criteria of this edition, the transcriptions retain the original note values. As with most of secular music of the time, Marenzio’s madrigals of the Book IV a 6 are in u, implying a semibreve tactus. Thus, the pieces are barred at the semibreve, each modern measure containing one tactus.1 The only exception is n. 6, Crudel, perché mi fuggi, which bears the mensuration sign p. The reason for this is not entirely clear: the p could be due to the seriousness of the text, or to the pattern of breves and dotted semibreves followed by minims in the second part of the piece. Still, the number of syllabic minims and semiminims does strongly suggest a semibreve tactus; thus the piece has been barred at the semibreve as well.
