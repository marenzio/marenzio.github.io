A volume, or “book” (libro) of madrigals or canzonette consisted of twenty to thirty pieces published together within each of three to six partbooks, depending on the number of voices. Unlike in today’s modern editions (including MODE), the music was not available in score format, that is, with all the individual parts aligned within a single page. So, all madrigals editions, including those so far published on paper, alter significantly the original object (more so than in a Mozart symphony). Publishers typically issued a few hundred copies of each of Marenzio’s first editions and reprints, and the few that survive today, scattered across European and American libraries, are often available in only one or two partbooks (for example, the soprano and bass parts might be found in one library, while the other parts need to be found in another library).
